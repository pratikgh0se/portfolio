Customer Found Bugs (CFDs) Reduction & Observability Enhancement

Company: ThoughtSpot

Dates: Nov 2021 - April 2024

Reduced Customer Found Bugs (CFDs) by 20% by standardizing dashboards and alerting in Grafana and Kibana. Cut logging cost by 38% by detecting log overflows and decommissioning obsolete metrics.

Key Technologies & Tools

Grafana, Kibana, Prometheus, Telegraf, New Relic, OpenTelemetry, Slack, Jira, Google Drive, Glean Search, LLMs, RAG, CRDs, Operator Pattern, Go, Python.

Situation

ThoughtSpot was experiencing a high volume of Customer Found Bugs (CFDs), which indicated significant gaps in our proactive monitoring and alerting capabilities. This reactive posture led to customer dissatisfaction, increased support burden, and prolonged Mean Time To Resolution (MTTR) for critical issues. Concurrently, our observability costs were escalating rapidly due to inefficient data retention policies and a proliferation of unused logs and metrics across our systems, leading to unnecessary storage and processing expenses.

Task

The multifaceted task involved: (1) Significantly reducing CFDs by enhancing proactive monitoring, standardizing observability practices, and fostering a culture of early issue detection. (2) Optimizing logging and metric costs by identifying and eliminating redundant or unused data. (3) Designing and building an automated Root Cause Analysis (RCA) tool to accelerate incident resolution and improve post-incident learning.

Action

  • Spearheaded a comprehensive "proactive bug hunt" initiative, including collaborative audits with engineering teams and implementation of templated Grafana dashboards based on RED and USE methodologies.
  • Developed Kubernetes Custom Resource Definitions (CRDs) and an Operator pattern in Go to automate the creation and management of Service Level Indicators (SLIs) and Service Level Objectives (SLOs) dashboards and alert rules in Grafana.
  • Collaborated with on-call and development teams to review and refine existing alerts, systematically eliminating noisy, unactionable, or duplicate alerts, reducing alert fatigue by over 50%.
  • Established a sophisticated alerting system that proactively identified services generating excessive log volumes, leading to a 27% reduction in overall log size.
  • Devised and implemented Python scripts to analyze metric usage across dashboards and alerts, identifying and decommissioning obsolete metrics, resulting in a 38% cost reduction.
  • Developed a custom Telegraf plugin to gain granular insights into log file growth and attribute costs more accurately.
  • Designed the comprehensive architecture for "Pharos," an automated Root Cause Analysis (RCA) tool, integrating diverse data sources like Prometheus metrics, Kibana logs, Slack conversations, and Jira issues.
  • Built a sophisticated Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system at the core of Pharos, leveraging a Large Language Model (LLM) to generate comprehensive, human-readable RCA reports.
  • Deployed Pharos in the development environment and integrated it seamlessly with Slack.
  • Conducted thorough architecture and security reviews, implementing code changes to reduce vulnerabilities and security threats.

Result

  • Substantial 40% reduction in Customer Found Bugs (CFDs) over the subsequent year, significantly improving customer satisfaction.
  • Logging costs were cut by 38%, and overall metric production by 38%, resulting in significant annual cost savings.
  • The "Pharos" RCA tool accelerated incident resolution by an estimated 30%.
  • Standardized dashboards improved system health visibility and application performance across the board.

Review

This was a highly impactful and transformative project that directly addressed critical business objectives: improving customer satisfaction and enhancing operational efficiency. My leadership in driving the proactive bug hunt, combined with deep technical skills in observability (Grafana, Kibana, Prometheus, Telegraf), Kubernetes automation (CRDs, Operators), and cutting-edge AI/ML (RAG for Pharos), was absolutely critical. The ability to collaborate effectively across multiple engineering teams and translate complex technical insights into actionable cost savings demonstrated strong business acumen and a holistic understanding of the platform. The successful development and deployment of Pharos showcased significant innovation in leveraging AI for complex operational challenges, setting a new standard for incident management at ThoughtSpot.
